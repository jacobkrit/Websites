    • Koudoumas Monastery

    Somethings to know before you go there!

    Koudoumas Monastery (Moni Koudouma in Greek) is a male monastery founded by the monks Parthenios and Eumenios, on the few ruins of an older 14th-century monastery (Venetian Period). Venetian documents refer to the old monastery as the “Monastery of Christ”, but it was later abandoned, perhaps due to pirates, and fell into ruin. When Parthenios and Eumenios came to Koudoumas, only the small church of the old monastery remained. This became the monastery church of Koudoumas Monastery. The construction of Koudoumas Monastery took from 1878 to 1895, with money and voluntary work offered by the local inhabitants. The craftsmen and all those who helped in the building of the monastery lived in the caves in the west wall of the gorge, opposite the monastery and higher up. There have been many additions and repairs to Koudoumas Monastery from that day to this. It contains two churches: the monastery church dedicated to the Virgin and that in honour of Saints Parthenios and Eumenios, whose feast-day is celebrated on 10 July. Crowds of people visit the monastery on that date and on 15 August, the festival of the Dormition of the Virgin, to worship and participate in the great festival.

    Tip:Ask for the 14th Century Venetian monastery.

    Αvvakospilio Cave

    See where orthodox-hermits lived (100 years ago)

    Walk to Αvvakospilio Cave, an easy half-hour walk to the west. The path begins behind the west cells and follows the shore. The Avvakospilio is also known locally as Varvakospilio (Falcon’s Cave), but this is folk etymology. It was called Avvakospilio after the many hermits (avvades) who lived there.

    Tip:Ask how many they used to be (hermits).

    Welcome at Aris

    In the south of Crete , about 2 kilometers east of Lentas , the tavern & inn Anatolis with its eight beautifully decorated guest rooms is located in a quiet location directly on the beach Petrakis. The Tavern & Inn Anatolis is visited by tourists and locals alike, as it offers always a fresh kitchen. On the menu card you will find Lentas lamb from their own brick oven, charcoal-grilled chicken and fresh fish caught himself. At the cozy bar you could get wine from the region in a relaxed, romantic atmosphere. For overnight guests, the tavern has prepared a rich buffet for breakfast. Of the tavern its just a few steps to the beach. Down the beach you will find sunbeds, parasols and a beach shower, but also a lot of lonely spots ... As well the tavern, including beach is a stage a month or twice: Cretan temperament pure with live traditional music, folklore, Lyra, Mandolin or Rembetika in front of a wonderfull starry backdrop. Enjoy the enchanting landscape of southern Crete at Petrakis beach. The best time for your holiday knows our own weather-station.

    You can visit the site Petrakisbeach.com/en/.

    TRIPITI

    Tripiti takes its name from the church of the Panagia Trypitis: built in a cave (=hole=trypa) in the gorge. The beach is extensive, but not affected by the strong local winds: it has sand, small pebbles and green crystal-clear waters which deepen rapidly. In the cliff-faces all around numerous caves have formed.
